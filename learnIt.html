Learn It

Done as part of my IMED classes in Patterns and Context, this app mockup was created to find a usable way for users who are interested in doing their own car maintenance to learn and check parts on an all-in-one app.

Overview

As part of a freeform, semester-long project covering the research and user interface creation of new applications, this site went through multiple iterations to achieve ease of use and provide a high level of knowledge for users.

Problem

As someone who has had bad experiences with working on cars and finding helpful advice on them. I wanted to create a place where people who are looking to learn the basics can not only learn but ensure they don’t start projects unprepared. With this I wanted to ensure the program had a checklist of tools before starting the project as well as ensuring the videos shown to the users are high quality and accurate.

Solution

The Learn It mobile application mockup was developed after research revealed a gap in the market. I identified a need for an all-in-one app that not only provided a checklist of parts and tools necessary for car maintenance but also offered quick and timely help to users. Additionally, the app aimed to feature community-generated videos, which would be thoroughly checked for accuracy and quality to foster a transparent learning environment.

Deliverables

Mockups The original mockups for the application. Designed over a 3-week period considering the professor’s requirements for the project as well as their feedback.

Final Protypes

Using Figma the protypes were created. This time ultiazing the color. As well as incorporating feedback and an improved video playback area and Forum section.

Outcomes

While this was just a class project, I gained significant insight into user context and how to design around specific user needs. I also learned a lot about identifying a demand for new applications and then crafting a solution. I'm excited to share that I'm continuing to develop this app into a functional prototype.
