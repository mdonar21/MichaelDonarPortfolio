Overview

As a group of three, we explored ways to increase student engagement within the student union. We did this by utilizing music creation programs in a method that could be reusable and expandable for future events.

Problem

The Student Union has often been seen as an afterthought within the university, offering not much outside of the usual quiet workplace for students to work. While a piano is available, we wish to bring a project that offers a lower barrier to entry.

Resreach

For our research, we began by analyzing similar music creation apps and JavaScript tools to ensure the project's feasibility within the timeframe. This included studying soundboard applications controlled via keyboard, as well as Windows programs offering comparable features. Following this digital exploration, we then focused on adapting the project into a physical installation at the student union. Our plan involved leveraging existing projectors, speakers, and tables to create a MIDI-based sound system for user interaction. Our market analysis revealed significant gaps in current digital and physical music experiences. We looked at intuitive sound control tools such as Bleep Drum, Kaoss Pad, Patatap, and Bloom, noting that they often lack integration or require subscriptions. Finally, interactive exhibits like Studio KÃ¤mp and Supersynthesis demonstrated a strong demand for immersive art but didn't support in-person musical collaboration with advanced features. Loop Labs aims to bridge these gaps. Our goal is to combine public interactivity, live sound manipulation, and accessible design into a single, hybrid platform that offers a unique and engaging music creation experience.

Solution

Loop Labs. A simple, yet powerful music-making device. With this application we wanted a way for students to get toghter and make music that would be heard all over the college not just in the union through a simple yet powerful music creation tool.
